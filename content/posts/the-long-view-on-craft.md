Craft is measured in decades, not quarters. It is the accumulation of careful choices, of instincts sharpened by repetition.

The craftsperson chooses the boring solutions: the ones that survive refactors, carry a team, and leave room for the next idea.

The long view is generous. It lets you change your mind, simplify the system, and still be proud of the work.

If you want to invest in craft, invest in feedback loops. The loop is where the learning happens.
